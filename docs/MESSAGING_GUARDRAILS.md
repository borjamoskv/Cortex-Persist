# Messaging Guardrails

This document freezes the public vocabulary for CORTEX Persist so README, landing pages, SDK metadata, and docs do not drift apart.

## Category

CORTEX Persist is **verifiable memory for AI agents**.

It is not:
- another vector database
- an observability platform
- a compliance product

## Primary Terms

Use these in README openings, hero copy, metadata, and title tags:

- `verifiable memory`
- `decision lineage`

## Secondary Terms

Use these to explain or deepen the primary terms:

- `trust layer`
- `decision record`
- `exportable evidence`
- `verification membrane`

## Allowed Claims

These are supported by the current product and documentation when phrased carefully:

- `tamper-evident memory`
- `tamper-evident decision lineage`
- `hash-chained records`
- `Merkle checkpoints`
- `exportable evidence`
- `supports traceability and auditability`
- `sits alongside your existing stack`
- `built to reduce the risk of unverified output contaminating durable state`

## Allowed With Caveat

These need qualifying language such as `designed to support`, `helps teams`, or `by construction`:

- `audit-ready`
- `security-first`
- `regulated workflows`
- `trust layer`
- `verification substrate`

## Prohibited Until Proven

Do not use these in public product copy without new benchmarks or stronger published evidence:

- `state of the art`
- `O(1)` performance claims
- `mathematically proven`
- `operational truth`
- `compliance-ready`
- `fully compliant`
- `tamper-proof`
- `provably secure`
- `cannot be bypassed`
- `replaces your vector database`
- `replaces your memory stack`
- `eliminates the need for observability`

## Surface Rules

- README, landing hero, metadata: lead with `verifiable memory` and `decision lineage`
- Landing hero: explicitly name the buyer or workflow stakes when possible
- Architecture docs: `trust layer` is acceptable as architectural framing
- Operational docs and FAQ: prefer `decision record`
- Research, RFCs, manifestos: `verification substrate` is acceptable when clearly aspirational

## Tone

- Keep public copy sober, specific, and technical
- Put doctrine and maximalist language in research or manifesto docs, not in first-screen product copy
- Prefer `supports`, `helps`, and `designed to` over absolute guarantees
